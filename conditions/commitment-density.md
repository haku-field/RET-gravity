# commitment-density

This condition appears when
**words or actions acquire enough density to become events**.

Not because they are serious.
Not because they are sincere.
But because they can no longer remain weightless.

---

## What “Commitment” Means Here

Commitment here does not mean:

- promise
- obligation
- responsibility
- moral stance

It refers only to **binding density**.

A word or act passes a threshold
where it no longer behaves as a signal,
but as a change in the field.

---

## Density Without Intention

Commitment-density does not require intent.

It may arise from:

- repetition without escalation
- presence without withdrawal
- statements left standing
- actions not neutralized by context

Intensity is irrelevant.
Persistence is sufficient.

---

## Difference from Choice or Decision

A choice can be tentative.
A decision can be revised.

Commitment-density appears
when revision no longer restores lightness.

The act remains revisable in theory,
but not reversible in effect.

---

## Event Formation

Once commitment-density is present:

- words are no longer only descriptive
- actions are no longer only expressive
- silence is no longer empty

Each contributes to configuration change.

An “event” here is not dramatic.
It is simply **irreducible**.

---

## Relation to Weight

Weight does not arise from meaning.

It arises when accumulated density
exceeds what the field can absorb
without changing.

Commitment-density is one way
that accumulation occurs.

---

## Non-Observability

There is no clear point
where density crosses a line.

The condition is inferred only afterward,
when interaction patterns no longer reset.

By that point,
density has already taken effect.

---

## Non-Instruction Clause

This file does not recommend commitment.

It does not warn against it.

It only records that,
in some configurations,
**light actions cease to remain light**.
