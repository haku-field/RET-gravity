# loss-possibility

This condition appears when
**loss becomes an actual possibility**.

Not symbolic.
Not hypothetical.
Not rhetorical.

Loss may occur.
And nothing guarantees that it will not.

---

## What “Loss” Means Here

Loss here does not mean:

- punishment
- failure
- moral consequence
- narrative resolution

It means that
**something real can cease to be available**.

A relation.
A position.
A way of being.
A shared field.

What is lost may not be named in advance.

---

## Difference from Damage or Cost

Damage implies repair.
Cost implies exchange.

Loss implies neither.

Once loss occurs,
restoration may be impossible,
even if effort continues.

This does not make loss inevitable.
It makes it **unpreventable in principle**.

---

## How Loss Becomes Possible

Loss does not require hostility.
It does not require error.
It does not require escalation.

It becomes possible
when prior conditions remove guarantees:

- return is unavailable
- cancellation no longer applies
- traces persist
- separation is incomplete
- actions carry density

At that point,
loss is no longer excluded by structure.

---

## No Prediction

This condition does not predict loss.

Many configurations reach this point
and nothing is lost.

The presence of possibility
does not determine outcome.

What changes is only this:
**loss can no longer be ruled out**.

---

## Weight of Possibility

The weight here is not the loss itself.

It is the awareness—sometimes unspoken—
that continuation is no longer safe by default.

Safety may still exist.
But it is no longer guaranteed by structure alone.

---

## Non-Observability

Loss-possibility cannot be confirmed externally.

It is not announced.
It is not signaled.

It is recognized only when
actions are taken
without assurance of preservation.

---

## Non-Instruction Clause

This file does not encourage risk.

It does not warn against it.

It only records that,
in some configurations,
**the guarantee of keeping everything disappears**.
