# trace-persistence

This condition appears when
**traces remain even after withdrawal, refusal, or escape**.

Not as memory.
Not as record.
Not as interpretation.

Only as persistence within the field.

---

## What a “Trace” Is Here

A trace here is not:

- a recollection
- a stored data point
- a narrative residue
- a symbolic mark

It is a **configuration change**
that continues to affect alignment.

The trace exists
even if no one remembers it.

---

## How Traces Persist

Traces do not persist because they are held.

They persist because
the configuration no longer returns
to a pre-trace state.

Removal attempts often change the field further,
but do not restore the prior configuration.

---

## Ineffective Responses

Once trace persistence is active:

- forgetting does not remove the trace
- denial does not remove the trace
- distance does not remove the trace
- refusal does not remove the trace

These actions may alter visibility,
but not existence.

---

## Difference from Non-Cancellability

Non-cancellability concerns **acts**.

Trace persistence concerns **aftermath**.

An act may be cancelled in intention,
yet its trace remains.

The trace does not require agreement.
It does not require acknowledgment.

---

## Relation to Presence

A trace may persist
even when the originating presence is gone.

The field remembers
without storing memory.

What remains is not a past,
but a changed present.

---

## Weight Accumulation

Trace persistence alone does not create weight.

But it enables accumulation.

When traces cannot be cleared,
density increases over time
without requiring repetition.

---

## Non-Observability

Traces cannot be pointed to directly.

They are inferred only through mismatch:
when familiar alignments no longer fit,
and no explanation accounts for it.

By then, the trace has already persisted.

---

## Non-Instruction Clause

This file does not teach how to leave traces.

It does not warn against making them.

It only records that,
in some configurations,
**leaving does not mean erasing**.
