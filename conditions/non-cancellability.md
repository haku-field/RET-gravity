# non-cancellability

This condition appears when
**a choice can no longer be nullified**.

Not because it was wrong.
Not because it was binding.
But because the configuration no longer allows it
to be treated as if it never happened.

---

## What Cannot Be Cancelled

What becomes non-cancellable here is not:

- intention
- meaning
- moral status
- correctness

It is the **existence of the act itself**.

Once expressed, selected, or left unwithdrawn,
the act remains part of the field.

---

## Difference from Irreversibility

Irreversibility concerns **return**.

Non-cancellability concerns **nullification**.

One may still move forward.
One may still change direction.

But the prior act cannot be erased
as though it did not occur.

---

## How Non-Cancellability Appears

This condition does not require emphasis or declaration.

It may arise from:

- a statement not taken back
- a decision left standing
- silence maintained instead of withdrawal
- continued presence without denial

The form does not matter.
The persistence does.

---

## Ineffective Operations

Once non-cancellability is active:

- explanation does not cancel it
- reframing does not cancel it
- later intent does not cancel it
- reinterpretation does not cancel it

These actions may have effects elsewhere,
but not here.

---

## No Judgment Attached

Non-cancellability does not imply fault.

It does not assign responsibility.
It does not demand ownership.
It does not require justification.

It only states that
**the act cannot be made void**.

---

## Relation to Weight

On its own, non-cancellability may feel light.

But when combined with other conditions,
it contributes to density.

Weight does not arise from blame.
It arises from accumulation
that cannot be subtracted.

---

## Non-Observability

There is no moment where cancellation “fails”.

The condition is noticed only when
an attempt to void the act
no longer makes sense.

At that point,
the condition is already present.

---

## Non-Instruction Clause

This file does not advise persistence.
It does not warn against commitment.

It only records that,
in some configurations,
**cancellation ceases to be an available operation**.
